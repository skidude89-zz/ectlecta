Hungarian Mushroom Soup

SUB TITLE

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 large onion - diced
  • 2 lbs Cremini Mushrooms
  • 1 tablesoon of paprika
  • 3 tablespoons of all purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of mustard powder
  • 1 tablespoon of chilli powder
  • 3 cups chicken stock (<--- Link to our article about stock, and why should make it.
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of worcestershire sauce
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 3/4 cup of sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 3 teaspoons of dried dill
  • 1 cup of chopped fresh parsley - plus more for serving
  • 2 tablespoons of beer - any beer will work but a stout is preferred
  • Salt to taste
  • Pepper to taste
  • Scallions - chopped (optional)
  • Bacon bits (optional)

Tools

  • Dutch Oven
  • Immersion Blender

Notes:

Total time - 2 Hours - Active time - 45 minutes

  • This can easily be made vegetarian if vegetable stock is used - great for Meatless Monday meals.
  • You do not have to blend the soup, it will still taste great but just have a thinner texture with large pieces of mushroom.
  • If you have leftovers, allow them to cool and place them in an airtight container in the fridge. It will keep for 3-4 days. To reheat either microwave or heat in a sauce pan.
  • This soup does not freeze well as it has cream in it, if you do freeze it be sure to adjust spices and add more cream as you heat it to help it return to the proper texture.

STEP #

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 Large Onion (Diced)
  • 2# Mushrooms

Tools

  • Dutch Oven

Melt the butter in the pan over medium heat. Add in the onions and mushrooms. Cook until the mushrooms have released all of thier liquids and it has evaporated. Stir frequently to ensure that the nothing sticks. This takes 15-20 minutes.

Once the mushrooms are done remove a cup of mushrooms and set aside.

NOTE: If you are doubling the recipe - or just prefer more mushrooms than suggested this may take much longer. I have made a double batch and having about 4 pounds of mushrooms in the pot made the evaporation process take about 45 minutes. You could do the mushrooms in batches and that would speed it up but I found that too complicated so I just put them all in together.

STEP #2

Ingredients

  • 3 Tbsp All Purpose Flour
  • 1 Tbsp Paprika
  • 1 Tbsp Chilli powder
  • 2 tsp Garlic powder
  • 1 tsp Mustard powder
  • 3 cups Stock
  • 1 cup Heavy cream
  • 2 Tbsp Soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce

Mix the flour and spices together. Add spice mix to onions and mushrooms and allow to cook for 1-2 minutes.

Add in the stock, soy sauce, worcestershire sauce and the heavy cream. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.

STEP #3

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup Sour cream
  • 1 Tbsp Lemon juice
  • 3 tsp Dry dill
  • 3/4 cup Parsley
  • 2 Tbsp Beer
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Mix the sour cream, lemon juice, dill, parsley and beer into the soup. Stir to combine.

Season with salt and pepper to taste and simmer for 5 mintues on low heat.

STEP #4

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Mushrooms (set aside in step 1)
  • 1/4 cup Parsley
  • Scallions (optional)
  • Bacon bits (optional)

Tools

  • Immersion blender

If you are using an immersion blender, blend until desired consitency is reached. I generally blend for about 1 minute, blending most of the soup into a creamy base but leaveing a few mushrooms intact. However, if you prefer you could blend the soup until creamy. That is a matter of taste / texture.

If you are using a traditional blender, blend the soup in batches until desired consistency is reached.

Once blending is complete, roughly chop the cup of mushrooms you set aside and add into the soup. Stir to combine. Allow to simmer for 3-5 mintues.

Serve topped with chopped parsley, scallions and bacon bits and additional salt and pepper to taste.
